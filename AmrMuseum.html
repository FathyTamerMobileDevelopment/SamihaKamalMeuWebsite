The Samihah Kamel Palace
It is located in the Zamalek district of Cairo, built in 1320 AH / 1902 AD, and was later purchased by Princess Samihah, daughter of Sultan Hussein Kamel, who had a deep love for the arts and recommended that the palace be dedicated to cultural purposes. The palace was transformed into the Greater Cairo Library in 1995.
A palace located in the Zamalek district of Cairo.
It was built by a Jewish grain merchant named Qattawi Pasha, who caught the admiration of Princess Samihah Hussein Kamel, daughter of Sultan Hussein Kamel. She purchased it from the merchant in 1942 and made some modifications to the palace to suit her taste.
Description of the palace
The palace consists of three floors and one underground level, surrounded by a magnificent iron fence. Its main gate is adorned with three intricately designed arches framed by pointed arches in an Andalusian style, topped with two iron columns that end in iron lanterns used to illuminate the gate at night. A marble staircase leads up to the entrance of the palace, where you are welcomed by the grand hall featuring three porticoes that form three sides of a square, reminiscent of the porticoes of the Mosque of CÃ³rdoba in Andalusia. These are Andalusian-style porticoes with luxurious pink marble columns topped with ornate capitals, above which are intricately designed arches framed by pointed shapes. The undersides of the arches and the capitals of the columns are decorated with floral motifs painted in gold. The palace has four facades, and perhaps the most striking feature is the circular tower that rises above the facades, located at the southern corner of the palace, where a series of magnificent windows cover its entire exterior.
